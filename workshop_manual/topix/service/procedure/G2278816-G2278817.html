Inspection and Verification
CAUTION:

Diagnosis by substitution from a donor vehicle is NOT acceptable. Substitution of control modules does not guarantee confirmation of a fault, and may also cause additional faults in the vehicle being tested and/or the donor vehicle.

NOTES:

  • If a control module or a component is suspect and the vehicle remains under manufacturer warranty, refer to the Warranty Policy and Procedures manual, or determine if any prior approval programme is in operation, prior to the installation of a new module/component.

  • When performing voltage or resistance tests, always use a digital multimeter accurate to three decimal places, and with an up-to-date calibration certificate. When testing resistance always take the resistance of the digital multimeter leads into account.

  • Check and rectify basic faults before beginning diagnostic routines involving pinpoint tests.

  1. Verify the customer concern
  1. Visually inspect for obvious signs of damage and system integrity

Fuel Fire Booster Heater (FFBH) Performance Issues

Diagnostic Procedure

A customer may express a concern that the vehicle passenger compartment fails to reach the selected temperature upon start up, or when the park heat function is used. Diagnostic Trouble Codes (DTCs) B1D2671 & B1D2993 may be stored. The cause of this could be that the air intake system of the Fuel Fired Booster Heater (FFBH) can become obstructed with the ingress of road debris such as snow/mud/salt

CAUTION:

This procedure requires a minimum of SDD 153.02 and Software Management Pack 294 loaded or later

NOTE:

The Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) Approved Diagnostic Equipment will read the Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) for the vehicle and automatically take the vehicle out of ‘Transportation mode’ if required

  1. Connect the Jaguar Land Rover Approved battery support unit
  1. Connect the Jaguar Land Rover Approved Diagnostic Equipment to the vehicle and begin a new session
  1. Follow the on screen prompts of the Jaguar Land Rover Approved Diagnostic Equipment
  1. Using the Jaguar Land Rover Approved Diagnostic Equipment, read the stored Diagnostic Trouble Codes (DTCs)
  2. Review the DTCs and address any unrelated issues before continuing with this procedure
  3. DTCs related to this procedure will be B1D26-71 and B1D29-93 and the DTCs will be stored in the HVAC Control Module. If there are any other DTCs stored, they should be investigated and rectified before continuing with this procedure.
  1. Clear the stored DTCs using the Jaguar Land Rover Approved Diagnostic Equipment
    NOTES:

    • The Fuel Fired Booster Heater may also be referred to as the Auxiliary Heater Control Module (AHCM) in the TOPIx Workshop Manual

    • The AHCM activation test should only be started when the engine is cool

    • The AHCM activation test should be performed with the engine running and with the climate controls set to the maximum temperature setting and maximum fan speed

    • This will operate the FFBH for 15 minutes

  1. Complete an AHCM activation test (0x900A). Refer to section 100-00. REFER to: Diagnostic Trouble Code Index - DTC: Automatic Temperature Control Module (ATCM) (100-00, Description and Operation).
  1. Follow the on screen prompts of the Jaguar Land Rover Approved Diagnostic Equipment
  2. Using the Jaguar Land Rover Approved Diagnostic Equipment, perform routine Auxiliary heater - Auxiliary heater (AHCM) activation test
  3. The application can be found as follows:
  4. Select the "Diagnosis" session type
  5. Select any of the following symptoms:
  6. Electrical - Climate control system - Auxiliary heating - will not heat to require temperature
  7. Run "Auxiliary heater - Auxiliary heater activation test"
  1. Follow the on screen prompts of the Jaguar Land Rover Approved Diagnostic Equipment
  1. Using the Jaguar Land Rover Approved Diagnostic Equipment, read any stored DTCs
  2. If the Fuel Fired Booster Heater (FFBH) actuated correctly during the "Auxiliary Heater Activation Test", and there are no DTCs stored, Continue to "Service Instruction 1" when this diagnostic procedure is complete.
  3. If the Fuel Fired Booster Heater (FFBH) did not actuate correctly during the "Auxiliary Heater Activation Test", and / or either DTCs B1D26-71 and B1D29-93 are stored, Continue to "Service Instruction 2" when this diagnostic procedure is complete.
  1. If necessary, clear the stored DTCs using the Jaguar Land Rover Approved Diagnostic Equipment
  1. If required, reset the vehicle to "Transportation Mode"
  1. When all of the tasks are complete, exit the session by selecting the "Session" tab and then select the "Close Session" option
  1. Disconnect the Jaguar Land Rover Approved Diagnostic Equipment and the Jaguar Land Rover Approved battery support unit

Fuel Fired Booster Heater (FFBH) System Functionality check

Repeat The AHCM Activation Test

This Section will carry out the AHCM activation test again, with the new components that have been installed

NOTES:

  • The Fuel Fired Booster Heater may also be referred to as the Auxiliary Heater Control Module (AHCM) in the TOPIx Workshop Manual

  • The AHCM activation test should only be started when the engine is cool

  • The AHCM activation test should be performed with the engine running and with the climate controls set to the maximum temperature setting and maximum fan speed

  • This will operate the FFBH for 15 minutes

  1. Complete an AHCM activation test (0x900A). Refer to section 100-00. REFER to: Diagnostic Trouble Code Index - DTC: Automatic Temperature Control Module (ATCM) (100-00, Description and Operation).
    CAUTION:

    This procedure requires a minimum of SDD 153.02 and Software Management Pack 294 loader or later

    NOTE:

    The Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) Approved Diagnostic Equipment will read the Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) for the vehicle and automatically take the vehicle out of ‘Transportation mode’ if required

  1. Connect the Jaguar Land Rover Approved battery support unit
  1. Connect the Jaguar Land Rover Approved Diagnostic Equipment to the vehicle and begin a new session
  1. Follow the on screen prompts of the Jaguar Land Rover Approved Diagnostic Equipment
  2. Click here to run: Auxiliary heater - Auxiliary heater activation test. (153.01 / 64320)
  1. If the hyperlink is not available, the application can be found as follows:
  2. Select the "Diagnosis" session type
  3. Select any of the following symptoms
  4. Electrical - Climate control system - Auxiliary heating - will not heat to require temperature
  5. Run "Auxiliary heater - Auxiliary heater activation test"
  6. Follow the on screen prompts of the Jaguar Land Rover Approved Diagnostic Equipment
  1. Using the Jaguar Land Rover Approved Diagnostic Equipment, read the stored Diagnostic Trouble Codes (DTCs)
  2. If the Fuel Fired Booster Heater (FFBH) actuated correctly during the "Auxiliary Heater Activation Test", and there are no DTCs stored, continue to "Step 8". when all steps are complete, release the vehicle
  3. If the Fuel Fired Booster Heater (FFBH) did not actuate correctly during the "Auxiliary Heater Activation Test", and / or either DTCs B1D26-71 and B1D29-93 are stored, raise a Technical Assistance (TA) and continue to "step 7"
  1. Using the Jaguar Land Rover Approved Diagnostic Equipment, clear the Diagnostic Trouble Codes (DTCs) that are stored in the HVAC Control Module
  1. If required, reset the vehicle to "Transportation Mode"
  1. When all tasks are complete, exit the session
  1. Disconnect the Jaguar Land Rover Approved Diagnostic Equipment and the Jaguar Land Rover Approved battery support unit
