Removal
NOTE:
  • This procedure contains some variation in the illustrations depending on the vehicle specification, but the essential information is always correct.
  • This procedure contains illustrations showing certain components removed to provide extra clarity.

  1. Raise and support the vehicle on a suitable 2 post lift.
    Refer to: Lifting (100-02 Jacking and Lifting, Description and Operation).
  2. Drain the rear differential.
    Refer to: Differential Draining and Filling vehicles without heavy duty differential (205-02 Rear Drive Axle/Differential, General Procedures).
    Refer to: Differential Draining and Filling vehicles with heavy duty differential (205-02 Rear Drive Axle/Differential, General Procedures).
  3. Remove the wheel and tire.
    Refer to: Wheel and Tire (204-04 Wheels and Tires, Removal and Installation).
  4. NOTE:

    Do not discard the nut.

    Remove the wheel hub nut.
  5. Remove the brake pad spring clip.
  6. Disconnect the Electric Park Brake (EPB) electrical connector and release the brake pad wear sensor.
  7. CAUTION:

    Make sure that the brake hose does not support the full weight of the brake caliper assembly.

    Release the brake caliper and tie aside.
  8. Release the wheel speed sensor.
  9. Release the height sensor link.
  10. Using a suitable hydraulic jack, support the lower control arm.
  11. Remove and discard the stabilizer bar nut.
    • Remove and discard the upper control arm to wheel knuckle nut and bolt.
    • Remove and discard the integral link nuts and bolts.
  13. Remove and discard the lower control arm to wheel knuckle nut and bolt.
  14. CAUTION:

    Do not use a hammer to detach the halfshaft from the wheel knuckle assembly, failure to follow this instruction may result in damage to the halfshaft.

    Using the special tool release the halfshaft from the wheel knuckle.
    Special Tool(s): 205-857
  15. CAUTION:

    Keep the halfshaft horizontal to avoid damaging the seal.

    Remove the halfshaft as illustrated.
  16. Remove and discard the circlip.
Installation
  1. Install a new circlip.
    Renew Part: Circlip Quantity: 1.
  2. CAUTION:

    Keep the halfshaft horizontal to avoid damaging the seal.

    Install the halfshaft.
  3. Install the halfshaft into the wheel knuckle.
  4. Install a new lower control arm to wheel knuckle nut and bolt.
    Renew Part: Lower control arm nut and bolt Quantity: 1.
    Torque:
    Stage 1: 110 Nm
    Stage 2: 60°
    • Install a new upper control arm to wheel knuckle nut and bolt.
      Renew Part: Upper control arm retaining nut and bolt Quantity: 1.
      Torque:
      Stage 1: 60 Nm
      Stage 2: 60°
    • Install the new integral link nuts and bolts.
      Renew Part: Integral link nuts and bolts Quantity: 1.
      Torque:
      Stage 1: 48 Nm
      Stage 2: 60°
  6. Install a new stabilizer bar nut.
    Renew Part: Stabilizer bar link lower nut Quantity: 1.
    Torque:
    Stage 1: 40 Nm
    Stage 2: 45°
  7. Remove the hydraulic jack.
  8. Use the original nut to install the halfshaft into the wheel knuckle.
  9. Secure the height sensor link.
    Torque: 9 Nm
  10. Secure the wheel speed sensor.
    Torque: 9 Nm
  11. Secure the brake caliper.
    Torque: 28 Nm
  12. Connect the Electric Park Brake (EPB) electrical connector and secure the brake pad wear sensor.
  13. Install the brake pad spring clip.
  14. Remove and discard the halfshaft nut.
  15. Install a new halfshaft nut.
    Renew Part: Rear halfshaft nut Quantity: 1.
    Torque:
    Stage 1: 80 Nm
    Stage 2: 60°
  16. Install the wheel and tire.
    Refer to: Wheel and Tire (204-04 Wheels and Tires, Removal and Installation).
  17. Fill the rear differential.
    Refer to: Differential Draining and Filling vehicles without heavy duty differential (205-02 Rear Drive Axle/Differential, General Procedures).
    Refer to: Differential Draining and Filling vehicles with heavy duty differential (205-02 Rear Drive Axle/Differential, General Procedures).
