GENERAL

This Pre-Delivery Inspection (PDI) manual is applicable to the Jaguar XE (X760) vehicles only.

The purpose of the PDI to make sure that the customer receives a vehicle which has been built to the highest possible standard.

Where possible, the PDI manual has been organized to make the PDI a more fluid and continuous process. This will allow the person conducting the PDI to prepare the vehicle in a more efficient manner.

References in the document text to Right-hand (RH) or Left hand (LH) side refer to the location of components when viewed from the driver’s seat and facing forward. Other references where RH or LH are used in text, are to describe the location of an item on/or within a component when facing the component.

Before starting the PDI, Service Bulletins must be checked for the latest information.

No vehicle should be put on public display before the PDI has been conducted.

Make sure vehicle body protectors and internal protection covers are available.

WARNING:

changes MUST NOT be made to the default vehicle configuration settings, unless specifically advised to do so.

Starting the Engine in Transit Mode

To start the engine before the vehicle has been taken out of Transit Mode, the following procedure must be followed:

  • Press the hazard switch to the ON position.
  • Press the hazard switch to the OFF position.
  • Turn the ignition ON.
  • Place the Smart key under the steering column (position displayed in instrument cluster).
  • Depress the brake pedal.
  • Start the vehicle.

RECTIFICATION AND WARRANTY

If simple rectification of a minor fault can be accomplished by the PDI technician, then this should be done (fluid reservoir top-up; touching in small chips or blemishes, etc.).

Major defects must be reported to Jaguar Land Rover North America, LLC / Jaguar Land Rover Canada ULC and rectified under strict supervision within the guidelines of the Warranty Policy.

Certain warranty work at PDI may require prior consultation.

Should items be missing, the required parts should be procured through normal channels and costs claimed back via the Dealer Direct Warranty (DDW) system under the ‘Shortage’ claim type. All missing items should be recorded on the PDI form and countersigned by an authorised person.

Claims for rectifying incorrect specification items and/or shortages must be made in accordance with the conditions detailed in Section C of the Warranty Compliance & Procedures Manual.

NOTE:

delivery damage is not the responsibility of Jaguar Land Rover Canada ULC / Jaguar Land Rover North America, LLC and must not be made the subject of a warranty claim. It is the responsibility of the Retailer to identify any such damage at the time of the new vehicle receipt and to make sure that the full details are recorded on the Delivery Receipt. Claims for rectification of such damage must be directed to the Delivery Company.

Failure to notify the Delivery Company of damage details at the time of vehicle delivery will result in claims for subsequent rectification being rejected.

Warranty claims for damage repairs may only be submitted where Jaguar Land Rover’s responsibility is clearly indicated. Examples falling into this category are paintwork damage during the fitment of trim or outward facing dents on the door skin.

Warranty claims will not be accepted for any damage repaired or identified after the vehicle has been placed into service.

SHOWROOM PREPARATION AND VALETING

The final preparation of the vehicle for the showroom or customer is of paramount importance. It is essential that the vehicle provides satisfaction to the most discerning customer. Failure to recognize this fundamental requirement will result in customer dissatisfaction and cause unnecessary work having to be carried out at a later date.

PRELIMINARY ADVICE

Symptom Driven Diagnostics (SDD)

During this Pre-Delivery inspection, certain sections of this manual require the use of the Jaguar Land Rover-approved diagnostics equipment Symptom Driven Diagnostics (SDD) software. Use the latest version of SDD available.

Transportation Protection and Seals

Make sure the vehicle has arrived with all transportation protection intact and that there is no obvious indication of an attempt to enter the vehicle. All transportation protection, except for the brake disc transit protection bags, are to be removed when satisfied that the vehicle has arrived in a sealed state.

Where the exterior protection item (full body cover or partial covers) are not intact or damaged in transit such that retaining the cover could cause damage to the vehicles bodywork, or present any other risk, then the cover or partial cover should be removed using the following guidelines.

Where minor damage is present, unlikely to affect the vehicles bodywork, or present any other risk, then guidance should be sought from the Jaguar Land Rover transport quality team in the first instance. Once any cover has been removed or damaged, it should never be refitted to a vehicle. Neither should a new cover be fitted to a vehicle during transit. The covers are designed to only be fitted in Jaguar Land Rover-approved locations. Where required, please contact the Jaguar Land Rover transport quality team for disposal instructions. Refer to Section ‘INTRODUCTION - 'RECTIFICATION AND WARRANTY’, for further details regarding warranty claims and liability.

All transportation protection and seals are to be removed when the retailer is satisfied that the vehicle has arrived in a sealed state. When removing the cover avoid any contact of the zippers, hooks, adhesive tapes, and dirty exterior areas of the cover with the painted surface. Remove the separate hood cover as last step to avoid scratches on the hood when removing the body cover.

Vehicle protection

Remove the vehicle protection.

CAUTION:

Take extra care not to damage the vehicle when removing the covers.

Vehicle protection removal:

  1. Remove the adhesive tape from the windshield.
  2. Unzip the drivers door panel and secure in an open position using the ties available.
  3. Open the drivers door and release the cover from the door sill and door striker. Release the hood latch.
  4. Close the drivers door, then zip the drivers door panel closed.
  5. Open the hood, release all the retaining hooks and other fixtures around the engine compartment.
  6. Release all the retaining hooks from the vehicle underbody, then release the cover from the front and rear bumpers.
  7. Release the cover from all the wheel arches.
  8. With assistance, from each side of the vehicle roll up the cover starting from the front of the vehicle. Lift the front part of the cover over the hood. When the A-pillars are reached, remove the cover from the exterior mirrors and roll the cover onto the roof.
  9. With assistance, roll the remaining part of the cover from the rear of the vehicle onto the roof.
  10. Remove the cover from the vehicle roof, make sure that the exterior part of sheet does not come into contact with the vehicle painted surface during this process.
  11. Release the zip, retaining hooks, and adhesive from the underside of the hood.
  12. With assistance, carefully lower the hood and roll the cover towards the windscreen before removing.

Additional handling instructions:

When driving the vehicle, the drivers door cover must be rolled up and fixed to the roof. During vehicle storage and transportation, the drivers door cover must be zipped closed.

Battery Condition

Make sure the correct standard of battery care is applied to the battery, For additional information, refer to: Battery Care Requirements (414-00 Battery and Charging System - General Information, Description and Operation).

Whenever any work is carried out a Jaguar approved power supply must be connected to the vehicle.

For requirements for PDI/delivery to the customer, refer to the battery care requirements. For additional information, refer to: Battery Care Requirements (414-00 Battery and Charging System - General Information, Description and Operation).

Battery voltage must be recorded during PDI and customer hand over. For additional information, refer to: New Vehicle Storage Form (100-11 Vehicle Transportation Aids and Vehicle Storage, Description and Operation).

Battery disconnect

If the battery is disconnected after conducting the PDI, certain vehicle electrical systems that are calibrated during the PDI will lose the calibration and will require resetting again once the battery is reconnected.

Touch Screen

The Touch Screen provides touch control of the vehicle Audio, Climate, Phone, Navigation, and Vehicle systems.

Throughout this document the console buttons surrounding the Touch Screen are referred to as hard-buttons and are to be pressed firmly. The Touch Screen menu buttons are referred to as soft-buttons and only require a short, light pressure to function. Do not use excessive pressure.

When operating the Touch Screen soft-buttons, always extend the tip of one finger and withhold the thumb and remaining fingers from the screen. Touching the screen with more than one finger at a time may cause false inputs.

Emergency Park Release System (EPR) - Automatic transmission vehicles only

In the event of a vehicle transmission failure, a mechanical means of selecting neutral is required; an Emergency Park Release (EPR) provides this functionality. The operating lever is located in the engine compartment, and is secured to a bracket on the intake manifold (2.0L Ingenium diesel and 2.0L GTDi petrol engines) or a bracket attached to the left-side of the supercharger (3.0L SC V6 petrol engine).

In an emergency, the EPR hand lever can be operated to release the park pawl within the transmission. The park pawl will not be allowed to re-engage until the hand lever is locked down in the closed position.
