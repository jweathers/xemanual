Removal
NOTE:

In this procedure the quarter panel is installed in conjunction with:

  • Rear door
  • Rocker panel moulding
  • Headliner
  • Side air curtain module
  • Rear window
  • Rear bumper
  • Rear armature
  • Rear lamp assembly
  • Loadspace trim panel
  • Rear seat cushion
  • Luggage compartment lid
  • Diesel exhaust fluid tank
  • Fuel tank filler pipe
  • Rear safety belt retractor
  1. The quarter panel is serviced as indicated.
  2. Before commencing this procedure make sure that you are aware of all Health and Safety requirements.For additional information, refer to: Health and Safety Precautions (100-00 General Information, Description and Operation).
  3. For further information on the methods, tools and fixings used in this procedure refer to the body repairs - general information section.For additional information, refer to: Body Repairs (501-25A Body Repairs - General Information, Description and Operation).
    4. NOTES:

    • Where breakstem fasteners (BSF) removal is instructed remove fastener shaft with a 4mm punch and remove the head with a 6.5mm drill bit. Clean all the drilled holes to make sure they are free from debris.

    • Where self piercing rivets (SPR's) removal is instructed use either the approved SPR tool or 5.3mm drill bit.

    • Any joints sealed as part of vehicle manufacture must be sealed as part of the repair.

  4. For additional information, refer to: Rear Door (501-03 Body Closures, Removal and Installation).
  5. For additional information, refer to: Rocker Panel Moulding (501-08 Exterior Trim and Ornamentation, Removal and Installation).
  6. For additional information, refer to: Headliner (501-05 Interior Trim and Ornamentation, Removal and Installation).
  7. For additional information, refer to: Side Air Curtain Module (501-20B Supplementary Restraint System, Removal and Installation).
  8. For additional information, refer to: Rear Window (501-11 Glass, Frames and Mechanisms, Removal and Installation).
  9. For additional information, refer to: Rear Bumper (501-19 Bumpers, Removal and Installation).
  10. For additional information, refer to: Rear Armature (501-19 Bumpers, Removal and Installation).
  11. For additional information, refer to: Rear Lamp Assembly (417-01 Exterior Lighting, Removal and Installation).
  12. For additional information, refer to: Left Loadspace Trim Panel (501-05 Interior Trim and Ornamentation, Removal and Installation) / Right Loadspace Trim Panel (501-05 Interior Trim and Ornamentation, Removal and Installation).
  13. For additional information, refer to: Luggage Compartment Lid (501-03 Body Closures, Removal and Installation).
  14. NOTE:

    Left hand only.

    For additional information, refer to: Diesel Exhaust Fluid Tank (303-08A Engine Emission Control - INGENIUM I4 2.0L Diesel, Removal and Installation).
  15. NOTE:

    Right hand only.

    For additional information, refer to: Fuel Tank Filler Pipe (310-01C Fuel Tank and Lines - V6 S/C 3.0L Petrol , Petrol, Removal and Installation).
  16. For additional information, refer to: Rear Safety Belt Retractor (501-20A Seatbelt System, Removal and Installation).
  17. Release and position the floor carpet to one side.
  18. Release and position the floor noise, vibration and harshness (NVH) material to one side.
  19. Release and position to one side the wiring harness along the inner quarter panel and rocker panel.
  20. CAUTION:

    Care should be taken not to cut through into the inner reinforcements.

    Mark and cut the quarter panel where the metal inert gas (MIG) welded butt joint is to be made as indicated.
  21. CAUTION:

    Care should be taken not to cut through into the inner reinforcements.

    Mark and cut the quarter panel where the MIG welded butt joint is to be made as indicated.
  22. Remove the SPR's as indicated.
  23. Remove the SPR's as indicated.
  24. Remove the SPR's as indicated.
  25. Carefully separate the quarter panel joints avoiding damage to the NVH components as indicated.
Installation
CAUTION:

Where structural adhesive is to be applied between surfaces it is essential compression is applied to the joints during the curing period.

NOTE:

BSF installation involves using the Jaguar approved BSF installation tool and requires a hole diameter of 6.5mm.

SPR installation requires using the Jaguar approved SPR tool.

  1. Remove the rivet remnants.
  2. Dress the flanges where necessary.
  3. Using the old quarter panel for reference, mark and cut the new quarter panel where the MIG welded butt joints are to be made as indicated.
  4. Offer up the new quarter panel and clamp into position. Check alignment if correct proceed to next step, if not rectify and recheck before proceeding.
  5. Drill holes where the BSF's are to be installed as indicated.
  6. Drill holes where the BSF's are to be installed as indicated.
  7. Drill holes where the backing plates are to be installed as indicated.
  8. Drill holes where the backing plates are to be installed as indicated.
  9. Drill holes in the new quarter panel where the backing plates are to be installed as indicated.
  10. Drill holes in the new quarter panel where the backing plates are to be installed as indicated.
  11. Remove the new quarter panel.
  12. Deburr the drilled holes.
  13. Measure and cut three 50mm wide backing plates, 25mm each side of the MIG butt joint from the quarter panel remnant.
  14. Cut four run on/run off tabs from the quarter panel remnant.
  15. Using a fine bristle disc clean and prepare the panel surfaces including the NVH components.
  16. Install and MIG plug weld the backing plates as indicated.
  17. Install and MIG plug weld the backing plates as indicated.
  18. Apply the coupling agent where the Jaguar recommended bonding material is to be applied and allow to dry.
  19. Apply a 5mm zig zag bead of the Jaguar recommended sealant as indicated.
  20. Apply the coupling agent where the Jaguar recommended bonding material is to be applied and allow to dry.
  21. NOTE:

    Make sure a continuous bead of adhesive surrounds fixing holes.

    Apply a 5mm zig zag bead of the Jaguar recommended bonding material as indicated.
  22. Offer up the new quarter panel and clamp into position. Check alignment if correct proceed to next step, if not rectify and recheck before proceeding.
  23. Tack MIG weld the butt joint as indicated.
  24. Tack MIG weld the butt joint as indicated.
  25. Install the SPR's and the BSF's as indicated.
  26. Install the SPR's as indicated.
  27. Install the BSF's as indicated.
  28. MIG plug weld the backing plates as indicated.
  29. MIG plug weld the backing plates as indicated.
  30. MIG weld the butt joint as indicated.
  31. MIG weld the butt joint as indicated.
  32. Dress all welded joints.
  33. Remove any excess adhesive.
  34. Cut off the run on/run off tabs.
  35. Carry out a non destructive crack inspection on the MIG butt joints. If correct proceed to next step, if not rectify and recheck before proceeding.
  36. Make sure that any open or exposed panel joints are correctly sealed.
  37. Make sure that all installed SPR's are sealed using a Jaguar recommended sealer.
  38. Make sure corrosion protection is applied to all areas affected by repair.
  39. The installation of associated panels and components is the reversal of removal procedure.
