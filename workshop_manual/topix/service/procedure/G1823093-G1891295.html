Inspection and Verification
CAUTION:

Diagnosis by substitution from a donor vehicle is NOT acceptable. Substitution of control modules does not guarantee confirmation of a fault, and may also cause additional faults in the vehicle being tested and/or the donor vehicle.

NOTES:

  • If a control module or a component is suspect and the vehicle remains under manufacturer warranty, refer to the Warranty Policy and Procedures manual, or determine if any prior approval programme is in operation, prior to the installation of a new module/component.

  • When performing voltage or resistance tests, always use a digital multimeter accurate to three decimal places, and with an up-to-date calibration certificate. When testing resistance always take the resistance of the digital multimeter leads into account.

  • Check and rectify basic faults before beginning diagnostic routines involving pinpoint tests.

  1. Verify the customer concern
  1. Visually inspect for obvious signs of damage and system integrity

Visual inspection

Mechanical
  • Brake pad(s) condition and installation
  • Brake caliper(s) condition and installation
  • Brake disc(s) condition and installation
  • Parking brake disc(s) condition and installation
  • Brake booster condition and installation
  • Brake booster vacuum hose condition and installation
  • Brake master cylinder condition and installation
  • Hydraulic Control Unit (HCU)
  • Brake fluid leaks
  • Brake warning indicator
  1. If an obvious cause for an observed or reported concern is found, correct the cause (if possible) before proceeding to the next step
  1. If the cause is not visually evident, verify the symptom and refer to the Symptom Chart, alternatively check for Diagnostic Trouble Codes (DTCs) and refer to the DTC Index
  1. Check DDW for open campaigns. Refer to the corresponding bulletins and SSMs which may be valid for the specific customer complaint and carry out the recommendations as required
Symptom Chart

Symptom Chart

SymptomPossible CausesAction
Brake noise
  • Foreign object(s) in brakes
  • Front/rear left/right brake pads excessively worn
  • Front/rear left/right brake disc(s) excessively worn
  • Check for foreign objects in the front and rear brakes
  • Check the front and rear brake pads for excessive wear
  • Check the front and rear brake discs for excessive wear
Brake vibration
  • Suspension components damaged
  • Front/rear left/right brake disc(s) excessively worn
  • Check the integrity of the front and rear suspension components
  • Check the front and rear brake pads for excessive wear
Excessive brake pedal travel/brake pedal spongy
  • Front/rear left/right brake pads excessively worn
  • Front/rear left/right brake pads not bedded in
  • Air in the brake system
  • Brake fluid leak (see visual inspection)
  • Brake master cylinder seal failure
NOTE:

Worn brake pads may make the pedal travel excessive. New brake pads may make the brake pedal feel spongy.

  • Check the front and rear brake pads for excessive wear
  • Check that the front and rear brake pads are bedded in
  • Check for a cause for air ingress
  • Check for brake fluid leaks
  • Check the operation of the brake master cylinder
Excessive brake pedal effort/brake pedal hard
  • Front/rear left/right brake pad(s) seized (in caliper)
  • Front/rear left/right brake caliper slide(s) seized
  • Front/rear left/right brake caliper piston(s) seized
  • Brake pipe(s) damaged/restricted
  • Brake vacuum pipe leaking
  • Brake vacuum pump internal failure
  • Brake booster internal failure
  • Check the front and rear brake pads for seizure
  • Check the front and rear brake caliper slides for seizure
  • Check the rear brake caliper pistons for seizure
  • Check the brake pipes for damage and restrictions
  • Check the brake booster vacuum pipe for air leaks
  • Check the operation of the brake vacuum pump
  • Check the operation of the brake booster
Low foot brake efficiency/brakes pulling/sticking/binding
  • Front/rear left/right brake pad(s) seized (in caliper)
  • Front/rear left/right brake caliper slide(s) seized
  • Front/rear left/right brake caliper piston(s) seized
  • Brake pipe(s) damaged/restricted/leaking
  • Front/rear left/right brake disc(s) excessively worn
  • Check the front and rear brake pads for seizure
  • Check the front and rear brake caliper slides for seizure
  • Check the rear brake caliper pistons for seizure
  • Check the brake pipes for damage, restrictions and leaks
  • Check the front and rear brake discs for excessive wear
Brake warning indicator illuminated
  • Brake fluid reservoir level too low
  • Brake fluid level sensor fault
  • Front/rear left/right brake pads excessively worn
  • Front/rear left/right brake pad wear sensor circuit short circuit to ground, open circuit
  • Check the brake pipes for damage and leaks
  • Check the operation of the brake fluid level sensor
  • Check the front and rear brake pads for excessive wear
  • Refer to the electrical circuit diagrams and check the brake pad wear sensor circuit for short circuit to ground, open circuit
