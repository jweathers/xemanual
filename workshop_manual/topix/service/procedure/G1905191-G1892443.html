OVERVIEW

The interior lighting system includes:

  • An approach lamp in each door mirror
  • Two footwell lamps for the passenger and driver
  • Glovebox lamp
  • Interior lamps in the front and rear overhead consoles
  • Two vanity mirror lamps in the sun visors
  • Door handle ambience lighting Light Emitting Diode (LED) (4 off)
  • Door armrest ambience lighting LED (4 off)
  • Door pocket ambience lighting LED (4 off) - if fitted
  • Ambience lighting LED in the floor console
  • Luggage compartment lamp
  • Front and rear footwell ambience lighting LED (4 off)
  • Treadplate illumination LED.

All of the interior lamps are controlled by the Body Control Module/Gateway Module (BCM/GWM) assembly.

The approach lamps, interior lamp, footwell lamps and rear interior lamps provide interior lighting, which operates automatically. The BCM/GWM assembly operates these lamps simultaneously when the vehicle is locked and unlocked, the doors open and close, the start/stop switch is pressed and the interior lamp is selected on and off.

The BCM/GWM assembly operates the luggage compartment lamp automatically when the luggage compartment lid is opened and closed.

The ambience lighting LED's in the floor console, on each door, footwell ambience lighting LEDs and in the luggage compartment operate with the interior lighting and with selection of the side lamps and headlamps. The following variants of ambience lighting are available depending on market and model specification:

  • Strong phosphor
  • True red
  • True white
  • True blue
  • Amber
  • Orange
  • Magenta
  • Lime green
  • Moonlight white
  • Daylight white.

The glovebox illumination lamp operates automatically when the glovebox door is opened or closed. The side lamps or headlamps do not need to be selected for the glovebox illumination lamp to operate.

The approach lamps are selected on and off by their related door module, in response to a Medium Speed (MS) Controller Area Network (CAN) body systems bus signal from the BCM/GWM assembly.

The interior and reading lamps are Light Emitting Diode (LED)s located on the printed circuit boards in the front overhead console. The lamps are manually controlled by a proximity sensing system or automatically controlled by the Body Control Module/Gateway Module (BCM/GWM) assembly.

The front interior lamp is switched on manually by placing a finger close to or touching the lamp lens to switch the lamp on or off.

The rear interior lamps are automatically controlled by the BCM/GWM assembly. The rear reading lamps are switched on manually by the switches.

When the doors are unlocked all of the interior lamps are illuminated. The lamps are extinguished 60 seconds after all doors are closed, when the engine is started or the vehicle is locked. The automatic illumination can be deactivated by touching the front interior lamp lens for 5 seconds. The automatic illumination is reactivated by touching the front interior lamp lens again for 2 seconds. The BCM/GWM assembly controls the interior lamp operation using a hardwired connection to fade the interior lamps on and off.

The reading lamps are switched on manually by placing a finger close to or touching the lamp lens to switch the lamp on or off. The reading lamps are provided a voltage feed from the power saver relay within the BCM/GWM assembly. This prevents excessive battery drain if one or both reading lamps are inadvertently left on.

Instrument Panel Dimmer Control

The instrument panel dimmer control rotary controller is located adjacent to the headlamp leveling control (when fitted) in the auxiliary lighting switch. The dimmer control provides a PWM output to control the illumination brightness of the Instrument Cluster (IC) backlighting, Touch Screen (TS) backlighting and ambience lighting.

The dimmer rotary thumbwheel is connected to a rheostat and a high side switch. The rheostat is a variable resistor which provides a high or low resistance according to its set position. This output is passed to a switchable capacitor or a high side switch. The high side switch uses the output from the rheostat to determine the switching frequency of the capacitor which provides the Pulse Width Modulation (PWM) output of between 8 and 12V to determine the brightness of the illumination.

Instrument Cluster

The Instrument Cluster (IC) does not use natural light to illuminate the display. The IC relies upon backlighting to illuminate the displays and separate illumination sources for the warning indicators. The level of illumination of the display has both a manual and automatic control.

NOTE:

The level of illumination of the warning indicators is not adjustable.

The manual control is provided by the IC dimmer control. The manual control enable the driver to adjust the level of illumination of the IC backlighting to their preference.

The automatic control is provided by light sensors in the IC. The automatic control adjusts the level of illumination for day or night. The switch between day/night mode is controlled by a set of timers and light threshold levels which uses the measured light sensor output. There are three speeds of transition between day and night used:

  • Slow speed transitions, are more than 2 minutes
  • Medium speed transitions, are more than 10 seconds and less than 2 minutes
  • Fast speed transitions, are less than 10 seconds.

Light Sensor

The light sensor measures the interior light levels that fall on the IC. The output is used by IC to determine when to change between day and night backlighting. The light sensor data is also transmitted by the IC for use by other systems on the vehicle, for example the infotainment system.

The light sensor are more sensitive to certain types of light, for example Light Emitting Diode (LED) lights.

Footwell Lamps

 
ItemDescription
1Footwell lamp (2 off)

A footwell lamp is located in each front footwell. The driver side footwell lamp is located in a bracket which is integral with the climate control system driver side floor duct. The passenger side footwell lamp is located in a bracket which is attached under the glovebox.

Each lamp comprises a halogen bulb installed in a bulb holder integrated into the vehicle instrument panel wiring harness. The footwell lamps are operated by a hardwired connection from the BCM/GWM assembly to fade the footwell lamps on and off with the interior lamps. If the automatic illumination function has been disabled, the footwell lamps are also disabled.

Approach Lamps

An approach lamp is installed in the underside of each door mirror.

Each approach lamp is powered by a fused permanent power supply to the Driver Door Module (DDM) and the Passenger Door Module (PDM) from the Passenger Junction Box (PJB).

Each door module receives a signal from the Body Control Module/Gateway Module (BCM/GWM) assembly on the Medium Speed (MS) Controller Area Network (CAN) body systems bus to illuminate or extinguish the approach lamp. The approach lamps are active when the vehicle is unlocked or the engine is switched off and a front door opened.

Vanity Mirror Lamp

 
ItemDescription
1Vanity mirror Light Emitting Diode (LED)

A vanity mirror lamp is located in each sun visor. The vanity mirror lamps are not connected to the Body Control Module/Gateway Module (BCM/GWM) assembly automatic interior lamp function.

The vanity mirror lamps are provided a voltage feed from the power saver relay within the BCM/GWM assembly. The lamps are provided with a power supply until the power saver relay contacts are opened. This prevents excessive battery drain if the vanity mirror lamps are inadvertently left on.

The vanity mirror is illuminated when the vanity mirror cover is fully opened.

Luggage Compartment Lamp

 
ItemDescription
1Luggage compartment lamp

A single luggage compartment lamp is located in the centerline of the parcel shelf.

Power is supplied to illuminate the luggage compartment lamps when the luggage compartment lid is opened and the luggage compartment lid latch ajar switch is operated. The switch supplies a ground signal to the Body Control Module/Gateway Module (BCM/GWM) assembly, which then illuminates the luggage compartment lamp. When the luggage compartment lid is closed the ground signal is removed and the luggage compartment lamp is turned off. The luggage compartment lamp is provided a voltage feed from the power saver relay within the BCM/GWM assembly. The lamps are provided with a power supply until the power saver relay contacts are opened. This prevents excessive battery drain if the luggage compartment lid is left open for a period of time.

Treadplate Illumination Light Emitting Diodes

The front door rocker panels are fitted with illuminated treadplates. The illumination is provided by Light Emitting Diode (LED)s. When the LEDs are active they illuminate a 'Jaguar' graphic within the treadplate.

The treadplate LEDs are operated via the automatic interior lighting controlled by the Body Control Module/Gateway Module (BCM/GWM) assembly. When the doors are unlocked all of the treadplate LEDs are illuminated along with the interior lamps. The treadplate LEDs are operated by a hardwired connection from the BCM/GWM assembly to fade the LEDs on and off with the interior lamps. If the automatic illumination function has been disabled, the treadplate LEDs are also disabled.

OPERATION

Interior Lighting

The interior lamp operation is activated by any of the following input signals:

  • front and rear door latches - door ajar switch contacts
  • Luggage compartment lid latch assembly - Luggage compartment lid ajar switch contacts
  • Power mode 4 - Accessory/vehicle unlocked
  • Central Door Locking (CDL) - Lock request
  • CDL - Smart key unlock request
  • Crash sensor input from Restraints Control Module (RCM)
  • Touching the lamp lens to switch the interior lamps.

The luggage compartment lamp is not switched on with the interior lamps. This lamp is only activated when the luggage compartment lid is opened. The luggage compartment lid open signal to the Body Control Module/Gateway Module (BCM/GWM) assembly is provided by a luggage compartment lid ajar switch in the luggage compartment lid latch assembly.

Automatic Control

The automatic control of the interior lamps has several on and off conditions as follows:

  • The interior lamps are on when one or more doors are opened. When all the doors are closed, the interior lamps are switched off after a delay of 60 seconds. If the vehicle is locked after closing the doors, within the 60 second period, the lamps go off immediately.
  • When the interior lamps are on and the ignition is in power mode 0 or 6, the lamps go off immediately after all doors have been closed.
  • In power mode 0, the interior lamps are switched on for 60 seconds. This timer is overridden if a door is subsequently opened.
  • When the vehicle is unlocked and the doors are closed, the interior lamps can be activated for a further 60 second period by pressing the 'unlock' button on the smart key.
  • When the vehicle is unlocked from a locked condition, the interior lamps are activated for a 60 second period. This timer is overridden if a door is subsequently opened.
  • If a door is left open, the interior lamps will remain on for 16 minutes. After this period, the Body Control Module/Gateway Module (BCM/GWM) assembly timer switches opens the contacts of the power saver relay and the interior lamps are switched to off to prevent excessive primary battery drain.

Manual Control

The interior lamps can be switched on or off using the manual switch for the front interior lamp. The front interior lamp switch will switch all front and rear interior lamps on or off. If the interior lamps are on when the switch is pressed, all interior lamps will go off. The on and off automatic switching conditions remain effective even when the manual switch is used.

In power mode 4 or 6, the front interior lamp switch is used to switch the interior lamps on, the lamps will remain on continuously. If the ignition is put into power mode 4, the interior lamps will be subject to the 16 minute Body Control Module/Gateway Module (BCM/GWM) timer. The interior lamp is immediately switched off when the vehicle is locked. After a locking procedure, the interior lamp automatic control is reinstated.

Continuous Off

To prevent drain on the primary battery if the doors are to remain open for some time, the interior lamps can be turned off permanently. This is stored in the Body Control Module/Gateway Module (BCM/GWM) assembly and is also activated following a system power down or a reset of the BCM/GWM assembly.

To enter the continuous off condition, the front interior lamp switch must be pressed and held for more than 5 seconds. The continuous off function can only be exited by pressing the front interior lamp switch for a further 5 seconds, after which, full automatic control is resumed. When continuous off is active, the front and rear interior lamp switches can still be used to switch the interior lamps on or off.

Crash Signal Operation

The Body Control Module/Gateway Module (BCM/GWM) assembly receives a crash signal on a hardwired connection from the Restraint Control Module (RCM) in the event of a crash of a severity to activate the airbags. On receipt of this signal, the BCM/GWM assembly activates the interior lamps which are on permanently and not subject to the BCM/GWM assembly timer.

The crash operation of the interior lamps can only be cancelled by operating the switch on the front interior lamp or by locking and unlocking the vehicle.

Ambience Lighting

The ambience lighting system has variable color options which can be adjusted by the customer using a menu option in the Touch Screen (TS).

The ambience lighting system uses red, blue and green Light Emitting Diode (LED)s which are combined together to make a range of ten different colors. In addition to the color of the ambience lighting, customers can also vary the brightness.

Automatic color changes are also activated when the driver selects dynamic mode. The ambience lighting automatically changes from the last user selected color to red (this color change is also repeated in the Instrument Cluster (IC) graphics).

Instrument Cluster Backlighting, Touch Screen Backlighting and Ambience Lighting Levels

The Instrument Cluster (IC) backlighting, Touch Screen (TS) backlighting and ambience lighting has three levels of illumination:

Side lights off/Day mode

Lighting levels between 60% and 100% of the maximum illumination level for the IC and TS.

  • The level of illumination of the IC and the TS is controlled automatically by the light sensors in the IC. A offset for the TS illumination can be set manually in Settings in the TS.
  • The instrument panel dimmer control is not operational.
  • The ambience lighting is off.
  • No switches are illuminated.

Side lights on/Day mode

Lighting levels between 60% and 100% of the maximum illumination level for the IC and TS.

  • The level of illumination of the IC and the TS is controlled automatically by the light sensors in the IC. A offset for the TS illumination can be set manually in Settings in the TS.
  • The ambience lighting is on. The illumination level is adjusted by the instrument panel dimmer control.
  • The switches are illuminated.

Side lights on/Night mode

Lighting levels between 10% and 60% of the maximum illumination level for the IC, the TS and the ambience lighting.

  • The level of illumination of the IC, the TS and the ambience lighting is controlled by the instrument panel dimmer control. A offset for the TS illumination can be set manually in Settings on the TS.
  • The switches are illuminated.
NOTES:

  • Side lights on is the same to the system, wether it is manually set to on by the driver or by the AUTO lights function.

  • When driving at night with the side lights off the minimum intensity of the IC is 60% of the maximum illumination level. This can cause significant glare for the driver.

For additional information, refer to: Instrument Cluster (413-01 Instrument Cluster, Description and Operation).

Stealth Mode

Stealth mode is set in the Settings on the TS. When the TS is set off and the IC is in night mode and the side lights are on.

In Stealth mode the illumination level of the IC and ambience lighting is set to the minimum illumination level of 10%.

Diagnostics

Diagnostics for the interior lamps is limited to a check of the function of the front interior lamp switch. The Body Control Module/Gateway Module (BCM/GWM) can be interrogated using a Jaguar approved diagnostic system to establish the correct operation of:

  • The front interior lamp switch
  • The door ajar switches
  • The luggage compartment lid ajar switch.
