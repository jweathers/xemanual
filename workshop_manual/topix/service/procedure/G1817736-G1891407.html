Removal
CAUTIONS:

  • Make sure that the road wheels are in the straight ahead position.

  • Make sure that the wiring harness is not twisted or damaged on removal. Failure to follow this instruction may result in damage to the vehicle.

  • Take extra care not to damage the wiring harness clips.

NOTES:

  • Removal steps in this procedure may contain installation details.

  • RHD illustration shown, LHD is similar.

  • Electric and manual adjusting steering columns with steering column lock actuator shown.


  1. CAUTION:

    Make sure the steering column is in the fully extended position to allow access to the steering column fixings.

  2. CAUTION:

    Make sure that the steering column lock actuator is not engaged and in the service position. Failure to follow this instruction will make installation of the steering system impossible and may result in damage to the vehicle.

    Refer to: Steering Column Service Position (211-04 Steering Column, General Procedures).
  3. Make the SRS system safe.
    Refer to: Supplementary Restraint System Health and Safety Precautions (100-00 General Information, Description and Operation).
    Refer to: Standard Workshop Practices (100-00 General Information, Description and Operation).
  4. WARNING:

    Make sure to support the vehicle with axle stands.

    Raise and support the vehicle.
  5. Remove the engine undershield.
    Refer to: Engine Undershield (501-02 Front End Body Panels, Removal and Installation).
  6. WARNING:

    Make sure that a new steering shaft connection bolt is installed.

    CAUTIONS:

    • Do not turn the steering wheel with the steering column lower shaft disconnected as damage to the clockspring may occur.

    • Fixings must be started by hand to avoid damaging threads.

    • Discard the bolt.

    • Care must be taken to avoid damaging the aluminium.

    NOTES:

    • Make sure to clean the threads prior to installation.

    • Note the steering column lower shaft to steering gear alignment marks.

    • The fixing must be fully tightened immediately following installation. Do not leave the fixing partially installed as adhesive may be activated.

    Renew Part: Steering column lower shaft lower bolt Quantity: 1.
    Torque: 30 Nm
  7. Refer to: Steering Wheel (211-04 Steering Column, Removal and Installation).
  9. CAUTION:

    Make sure that the clockspring rotor does not rotate.

    Secure with adhesive tape.

  11. Vehicles with manual steering column.

Installation
  1. CAUTIONS:

    • Make sure that a new steering shaft connection bolt is installed.

    • Fixings must be started by hand to avoid damaging threads.

    NOTE:

    The fixing must be fully tightened immediately following installation. Do not leave the fixing partially installed as adhesive may be activated.

    Torque: 22 Nm
  2. CAUTION:

    Make sure the washers are installed correctly.

  3. To install, reverse the removal procedure.
  4. CAUTIONS:

    • Make sure that the steering column adjust cable is correctly installed. Failure to follow this instruction may result in damage to the vehicle.

    • Check for correct operation of the steering column adjust cable.

    Vehicles with manual steering column.
  5. CAUTIONS:

    • Make sure that the steering column adjust cable is correctly installed. Failure to follow this instruction may result in damage to the vehicle.

    • Check for correct operation of the steering column adjust cable.

    Vehicles with manual steering column.
  6. If a new steering column with a new steering column lock actuator is fitted calibrate the steering column lock actuator as new module using the Jaguar approved diagnostic equipment.
  7. If a new electric adjusting steering column is fitted run the steering column calibration routine using the jaguar approved diagnostic equipment to ensure correct adjust function.
