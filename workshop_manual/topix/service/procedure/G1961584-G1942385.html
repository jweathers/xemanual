Make sure the vehicle has arrived in sealed state

Make sure that the vehicle has arrived with the transportation seals intact.

Record the vehicle details

Record the following details on the PDI form:

  • Customer name
  • Model
  • Registration number
  • Invoice number
  • Odometer
  • Vehicle Identification Number
  • Registration date
  • Smart key number
  • Engine number
  • Paint code
  • Trim code
  • Battery test code

Vehicle information locations

The VIN is displayed on a label that is visible in the lower left edge of the windshield (1) and stamped on the front right suspension top mount (2).

The VIN, paint code, trim code and manufacture date are shown on the vehicle certification label, affixed to the ‘B’ pillar (3).

Engine number location: 2.0L Ingenium diesel

Engine number location: 2.0L GTDi petrol

Engine number location: 3.0L SC V6 petrol

The engine number is visible from under the vehicle on the left side of the engine block.

Vehicle specification

Make sure the vehicle conforms to the specification and options required. Check that all accessories are included with the vehicle.

NOTE:

all equipment used must be functionally capable of meeting the compliance requirements.

Jaguar Land Rover-approved Midtronics Power Supply Charger and diagnostic tool

Connect the Jaguar Land Rover-approved Midtronics Power Supply Charger to the vehicle. Connect the positive (+) booster cable to the positive (+) charge point and connect the negative (-) booster cable to the negative (-) charge point in the engine compartment. Make sure the correct standard of battery care is applied to the battery.

CAUTION:

Failure to charge the battery in this manner will cause the Battery Monitor System (BMS) to NOT register the charge increase. This can cause certain systems, including the Stop/Start system, to be inhibited during the first few days of driving while the BMS re-calibrates.

Connect the Jaguar Land Rover-approved diagnostic tool to the vehicle.

Diagnostic connector

SDD PDI application

The PDI application will activate certain vehicle systems.

Run the SDD PDI application

  1. Select Read VIN
  2. SDD will read the vehicle identification details from the vehicle.
  3. Confirm that the vehicle details are correct.
  4. If the details are incorrect manually enter the vehicle identification details.
  5. Check the Vehicle Specifications read by SDD match the actual vehicle specifications.
  6. Ignition ON prompt will then appear.
  7. Make sure that both Smart Keys supplied with the vehicle are positioned on the centre console.
  8. Select the ‘PDI’ tab.
  9. Select the ‘Recommendations’ tab.
  10. From the menu, select ‘Pre-Delivery Inspection’ and click ‘RUN’.
  11. Follow the on-screen instructions. This will take the vehicle out of transit mode, sets the Service Interval Announcer (SIA), on petrol models ‘flight recorder’ data, and EMS adaption values.
  12. NOTE:

    if fitted: Wi-Fi must be OFF before the starting the telematics activation procedure.

    If the vehicle is equipped with telematics, confirm the market of the vehicle when prompted.
    1. The system will now search for signal coverage.
    2. If it detects a good signal coverage, confirm to activate the telematics module.
    3. A message will state if activation was successful, a red light will illuminate the SOS call button.
    4. If unsuccessful or SDD states a poor signal coverage, the vehicle must be moved to a area of good signal coverage.
    5. Press the information call button located on the overhead console, after 10 seconds the illumination pattern will change. Release the button and the system will try to activate the telematics module. Note: This will time out after 2 minutes.
    6. If successful, the information call light will stay illuminated for a short period and the SOS call button will be illuminated red.
    7. If unsuccessful, repeat the process until the SOS call button is illuminated red.

Navigation system

If required, a data storage device will require activating and then inserted into the vehicle.

The data storage device will be automatically activated if all conditions are correct (recommended process):

  1. If required, switch the ignition ON.
  2. Remove the correct data storage device for the vehicle, from its packaging.
  3. Insert the data storage device into the approved diagnostic tool data storage device slot.
  4. Follow the on-screen instructions to confirm the data storage device is in the approved diagnostic tool and it is connected to the network.
  5. When the map activation process is complete, one of the following screens will be displayed;
    • Activation successful and no map update available – proceed to Step 6.
    • Activation successful and a map update is available – proceed to Map Data Update if a map data update is to be performed (this is not part of the PDI process and can be performed at any time), or proceed to Step 6.
    • Activation unsuccessful and an error information will be displayed. Continue to follow the on-screen instructions.
  6. Follow the on-screen instructions to remove the data storage device from the approved diagnostic tool and insert the data storage device into the navigation data storage device slot in the vehicle.
  7. The navigation system will restart after confirming the data storage device is inserted in the vehicle correctly.
  8. When the restart is complete switch the ignition OFF and wait 20 seconds.
  9. Switch the ignition ON and select Navigation to confirm the map information is correct. NOTE: make sure the vehicle has sufficient Global Positioning System (GPS) signal, it may be necessary to move the vehicle to an area of good signal coverage.
NOTE:

If the telematics module fails to configure, contact Technical HelpLine to confirm the server is operational before attempting further diagnosis or repair.

Discard the data storage device packaging.

To activate multiple data storage devices (NOT RECOMMENDED):

CAUTION:

Data storage devices activated with a particular VIN will only work in the vehicle with that VIN.

  1. Insert the data storage device into the approved diagnostic tool and follow the on-screen instructions for activating the data storage device, entering the vehicles VIN where requested.
  2. The verification information will be saved to the data storage device.
  3. A message will appear to confirm that the data storage device has been activated or if a new map update is available.
  4. Download the new map update, if available and make sure the data storage device contains the latest map information.
  5. Repeat this process for multiple data storage devices if required.
  6. With the vehicle ignition switched ON and the Touch Screen switched ON, select the Home screen.
  7. Insert the activated data storage device into the Navigation data storage device slot.
  8. Select Navigation (press Retry if necessary), the head unit will restart.
  9. 9. Switch the ignition OFF and wait 20 seconds.
  10. 10. Switch the ignition ON and select Navigation, confirm the map information is correct.

Map Data Update

This update can be performed using the Map Updater, which is available from the approved diagnostic tool welcome main screen or any internet connected computer with the InControl Map Updater application installed.

  1. Insert the data storage device into the approved diagnostic tool or internet connected computer.
  2. Start the InControl Map Updater application and follow the on-screen instructions for updating the map data on the data storage device.
  3. The verification information will be saved to the data storage device.
  4. Switch the ignition OFF.

Disconnect the battery power supply and diagnostic tool from the vehicle.

Battery test

Check the battery charge condition and record the battery voltage on the PDI form.

Battery Access

Make sure that both the positive (+) and negative (-)battery terminals are correctly located and torqued to 6 Nm.

Surface charge removal

A vehicle which has had its battery charged or been driven in a 24-hour period before the test must have its surface charge removed.

  • Turn the ignition ON (do not start the engine).
  • Switch the headlamps ON high beam for a minimum three minutes.
  • Switch the headlamps OFF.
  • Wait a minimum of five minutes before recording test results for any battery measurements.

Battery test

The battery condition must be checked in accordance with the battery test process utilizing the Jaguar Land Rover-approved Midtronics battery tester.

NOTE:

The Midtronics code must be recorded on the PDI form.

Any actions must be carried out in accordance with the table shown in the determining battery condition section. The details must be recorded on the New Vehicle Storage Form which is part of the new vehicle storage document. For additional information, refer to: New Vehicle Storage Form (100-11 Vehicle Transportation Aids and Vehicle Storage, Description and Operation).

CAUTION:

Do not connect the tester negative (-) cable to any other circuit or chassis point other than the battery negative (-) terminal.

The battery must be tested to determine that the battery is in good condition, and there is sufficient charge to carry out the PDI. Certain electrical systems require a precise voltage to operate correctly.

  • Connect the battery tester to the battery.
  • Check the battery condition as indicated by the battery tester and record the results on the PDI form.
CAUTION:

The battery voltage check must be performed to make sure the Intelligent Stop/Start function performs correctly. The battery MUST be in good condition before delivery to the customer.

NOTE:

The voltage check must be carried out before connecting the diagnostic equipment. Failure to do this may result in vehicle configuration failure.

Connect the battery power supply.

Underhood fluid levels

Check and top-up underhood fluid levels. Investigate and report the cause of any low fluid levels. Check the correct fitting of all hoses and connectors, making sure nothing is loose.

When checking fluid levels, always thoroughly check the particular system for leaks from hoses, pipes, and reservoirs even though the fluid levels may be correct.

  2. Brake fluid reservoir - Left-hand drive (LHD) vehicles (beneath covers)
  3. Engine coolant filler reservoir
  4. Oil level gauge
  5. Engine oil filler cap
