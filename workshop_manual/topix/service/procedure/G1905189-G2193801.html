OVERVIEW

A number of antennas are used for the various frequency reception requirements of the infotainment system.

A rood pod is fitted and contains antennas for Digital Audio Broadcast (DAB), Global System for Mobile communications (GSM) and Global Navigation Satellite System (GNSS) and Global Positioning System (GPS).

At the top of the rear window are 3 antenna elements which are not part of the Heated Rear Window (HRW). These are marked with the word 'Antenna' next to them. The 3 antenna elements create 2 antenna structures that are used for Amplitude Modulation (AM) (left side) and Television (TV) (right side).

At the top of the rear window are 2 antenna elements which are not part of the HRW. The 2 antenna elements create 2 antenna structures that are used for TV (left side) and Fuel Fired Booster Heater (FFBH). The FFBH antenna element is used for FFBH remote control and is not part of the infotainment antenna system.

NOTE:

TV is only available in Japan market.

The HRW elements are utilized for TV, DAB, Frequency Modulation (FM), Traffic Message Channel (TMC).

DESCRIPTION

Roof Pod

A roof pod is fitted externally at the rear of the roof panel. The roof pod is secured to the roof panel with 2 studs, a mounting bracket and 2 nuts. A seal between the roof pod and the outer surface of the roof panel prevents the ingress of water.

The A surface of the roof pod is a separate cover that is clipped in place and painted to body color.

The roof pod can contain antenna for:

  • Global Navigation Satellite Systems (GNSS) (GPS all versions and additional GLONASS on non SDARS variants)
  • Digital Audio Broadcast - DAB-L
  • 2 x mobile phone antennas.
  • SDARS
  • WiFi
NOTE:

Global Navigation Satellite System (GLONASS) is the Russian global satellite system.

Depending on vehicle specification and market requirements, the roof pod is available with a combination of the above antenna options.

Rear Window Antennas

The Heated Rear Window (HRW) elements are utilized for Radio Frequency (RF) reception for Television (TV), Digital Audio Broadcast-3 (DAB-3), Frequency Modulation (FM), Traffic Message Channel (TMC) (when navigation system is fitted) and Vehicle Information and Communication System (VICS) (Japan Only).

Dedicated antenna elements, which are not part of the HRW, are located at the top and bottom of the rear window. The top elements are used for AM and TV reception. The bottom elements are used for TV and FFBH remote control.

Up to 2 antenna amplifiers are located on the vehicle body on each side of the rear window. The amplifiers are attached to the vehicle body with a screw and connected to the dedicated antenna elements or the HRW elements.

The forward left side antenna amplifier is used for AM/FM reception. The left side rearward antenna amplifier is used for TV reception.

The forward right side antenna amplifier is used for FM/Traffic Message Channel (TMC)/DAB-3 reception in ROW markets. For Japan market the antenna amplifier is used for TV reception. The right side rearward antenna amplifier is used for FM and VICS reception for the Japan market only.

A TV antenna amplifier is located on the left side below the rear window. The antenna amplifier is located on the parcel shelf panel and secured with a screw.

RF Filters

A RF filter is located on each side of the rear window. The RF filters are connected to the positive and negative connections for the Heated Rear Window (HRW) and remove interference created when the HRW is operating.

Global Positioning System Signal Splitter

The GPS signal splitter is located in the luggage compartment behind the right side rear seat. there are 2 studs attached to the floor pan center reinforcing panel provide for attachment of the GPS signal splitter which is secured with 2 nuts. The GPS signal splitter is only fitted to vehicles with the Telematics system.

The GPS signal splitter shares the GNSS signal (GPS and GLONASS) from the roof pod antenna with the Telematics Control Module and the Audio Head Unit (AHU)

For additional information, refer to: Navigation System (415-01B Information and Entertainment System - Vehicles With: InControl Touch, Description and Operation).

OPERATION

Diagnostics

The Audio Head Unit (AHU) can detect the connections between the tuners in the AHU and the AM/FM/DAB antenna amplifiers. Faults in these connections will store a Diagnostic Trouble Code (DTC) in the AHU.

The Digital Audio Broadcast (DAB) 3 and L-band and the GNSS antennas are monitored by the AHU. Faults with these antennas will record DTCs in the AHU.
