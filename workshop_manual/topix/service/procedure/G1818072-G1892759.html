OVERVIEW

Where fitted, the pedestrian protection system opens the hood approximately 130 mm (5.1 in.) if it detects an impact with a pedestrian while the vehicle is in the 25 to 50 km/h (16 to 31 mph) speed range.

The Pedestrian protection system comprises:

  • Two pedestrian protection system pressure sensors.
  • A pedestrian protection system pressure tube.
  • Two hood deployment controls.
  • The RCM (Restraints Control Module).
DESCRIPTION

Pressure Sensors

NOTE:

It is not possible to replace the pressure sensors or pressure tube as individual components, the sensors and tube are supplied as a single component.

The two pressure sensors are mounted on the right and left hand side of the front bumper. They are connected to each end of the silicon pressure tube which is held in position by a 3 piece foam assembly in the front bumper. The silicon pressure tube is held into a channel in the foam by clips.

The pressure sensors have the characteristics as follow:

  • Transmission of differential pressure data.
  • Transmission of status data during start-up.
  • Power-on sensor self-test.
  • Parity checksum to ensure safe data transmission.

Each pressure sensor is directly connected to the RCM (Restraints Control Module).

Silicon Pressure Tube

The silicon pressure tube has a visible blue mark at its mid-point that indicates the tube is installed symmetrically

Pressure Tube Foam

The pressure tube foam acts as a reaction surface for the silicon tube during an impact and defines the routing path for the tube. The 11 clips hold the silicon tube in the grove.

The hood deployment controls are located on the front inner fenders just forward of the hood latches. The hood deployment controls comprise a pyrotechnic piston lifter to raise the hood.

OPERATION

The pedestrian protection system is active at vehicle speeds between 25 to 50 km/h (15 to 31 mph).

Hood deployment controls are triggered by pressure sensors mounted on either side of the front bumper. The pressure sensors are attached to each end of a silicon pressure tube which is mounted on the inside of the front bumper. An impact creates a pressure pulse inside the silicon tube which is detected by the two pressure sensors. The pressure sensors transmit a digital status signal to the RCM which fires the hood deployment controls if the operating parameters are met.

The RCM receives information about the vehicle's current speed from the ABS (Anti-lock Brake System Control Module) on the HS (High Speed) CAN (Controller Area Network) powertrain systems bus. If the predetermined parameters are met the RCM fires the hood deployment controls which force the rear of the hood upwards. The raising of the hood creates a more cushioned effect between the vehicle and the pedestrian.

The RCM will send a signal to the BCM/GWM (Body Control Module/Gateway Module) on the HS CAN powertrain systems bus to activate the hazard warning lights. The hazard warning lights can only be switched off by pressing the engine start/stop button to turn the engine OFF and ON again (without pressing the brake pedal) to revert to the ignition convenience mode.

A warning message to check the pedestrian protection system will appear in the IC (Instrument Cluster). The vehicle must not be driven once the hood has been deployed.
