Rear Side Member Closing Panel Section (G1785006)

REMOVAL AND INSTALLATION

Removal
NOTE:

The rear side member closing panel is installed in conjunction with:

  • Rear bumper
  • Rear armature
  • Rear lamp assembly
  • Battery
  • Loadspace trim panel
  • Auxiliary junction box (AJB)
  • Subwoofer amplifier
  • Back panel outer
  • Back panel inner
  • Spare wheel well
  1. The rear side member closing panel is serviced as indicated.
  2. Before commencing this procedure make sure that you are aware of all Health and Safety requirements. For additional information, refer to: Health and Safety Precautions (100-00 General Information, Description and Operation).
  3. For further information on the methods, tools and fixings used in this procedure refer to the body repairs - general information section.
    4. NOTES:

    • Any joints sealed as part of vehicle manufacture must be sealed as part of the repair.

    • Make sure a zinc rich primer is applied to any bare metal surfaces.

    • To make sure that the vehicle is correctly aligned it must be placed on a Land Rover approved alignment jig.

  4. For additional information, refer to: Rear Bumper (501-19 Bumpers, Removal and Installation).
  5. For additional information, refer to: Rear Armature (501-19 Bumpers, Removal and Installation).
  6. For additional information, refer to: Rear Lamp Assembly (417-01 Exterior Lighting, Removal and Installation).
  7. For additional information, refer to: Battery (414-01 Battery, Mounting and Cables, Removal and Installation).
  8. For additional information, refer to: Loadspace Trim Panel (501-05 Interior Trim and Ornamentation, Removal and Installation) / Left Loadspace Trim Panel (501-05 Interior Trim and Ornamentation, Removal and Installation) / Right Loadspace Trim Panel (501-05 Interior Trim and Ornamentation, Removal and Installation).
  9. NOTE:

    Right hand only.

    For additional information, refer to: Rear Junction Box (418-00 Module Communications Network, Removal and Installation).
  10. NOTE:

    Left hand only.

    For additional information, refer to: Subwoofer Amplifier (415-01 Information and Entertainment System, Removal and Installation).
  11. For additional information, refer to: Back Panel Outer (501-30 Rear End Sheet Metal Repairs, Removal and Installation).
  12. For additional information, refer to: Back Panel Inner (501-30 Rear End Sheet Metal Repairs, Removal and Installation).
  13. For additional information, refer to: Spare Wheel Well (501-30 Rear End Sheet Metal Repairs, Removal and Installation).
  14. CAUTION:

    Care should be taken not to cut through into the inner reinforcements.

    Using a combination of the new rear side member closing panel and the measurements mark the position where the metal inert gas (MIG) welded butt joint is to be made and cut the panel as indicated.
  15. Remove the spot welds as indicated.
  16. Carefully separate the joints and remove the rear side member closing panel.
Installation
  1. Dress the flanges where necessary.
  2. Remove the spot welds and separate the new rear side member closing panel from the new service panel as indicated.
  3. Using the old rear side member closing panel as a guide mark, measure and cut the new rear side member closing panel where the MIG butt joint is to be made as indicated.
  4. Clean and prepare the panel surfaces.
  5. Offer up the new rear side member closing panel and clamp into position. Check alignment if correct proceed to next step, if not rectify and recheck before proceeding.
  6. NOTE:

    Make sure the holes are drilled through the rear side member closing panel and the rear floor side extension.

    Drill holes where the MIG plug welds are to be installed as indicated.
  7. Tack MIG weld the rear side member closing panel butt joint as indicated.
  8. Install the MIG plug welds as indicated.
  9. MIG weld the butt joint as indicated.
  10. Dress the MIG welded butt weld.
  11. Make sure that any open or exposed panel joints are correctly sealed.
  12. Make sure corrosion protection is applied to all areas affected by repair.
  13. The installation of associated panels and components is the reversal of removal procedure.
