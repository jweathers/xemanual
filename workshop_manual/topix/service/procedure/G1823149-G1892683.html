Inspection and Verification
CAUTION:

Diagnosis by substitution from a donor vehicle is NOT acceptable. Substitution of control modules does not guarantee confirmation of a fault, and may also cause additional faults in the vehicle being tested and/or the donor vehicle.

NOTES:

  • If a control module or a component is suspect and the vehicle remains under manufacturer warranty, refer to the Warranty Policy and Procedures manual, or determine if any prior approval programme is in operation, prior to the installation of a new module/component.

  • When performing voltage or resistance tests, always use a digital multimeter accurate to three decimal places, and with an up-to-date calibration certificate. When testing resistance always take the resistance of the digital multimeter leads into account.

  • Check and rectify basic faults before beginning diagnostic routines involving pinpoint tests.

  1. Verify the customer concern
  1. Visually inspect for obvious signs of damage and system integrity

Instructions For Cerium Oxide Windscreen Cleaning Procedure

Erratic movements of the wiper blades across the windscreen may be caused by contaminants or residue on the surface of the glass. Under these circumstances, the windscreen glass can be deep-cleaned using cerium oxide powder as detailed below:

  1. Before applying the cerium oxide powder, the wiper blades should be wrapped with paper roll to protect them.
  1. Lightly spray water onto the windscreen glass (this will allow the dry cerium oxide powder to adhere to the screen during the cleaning process).
  1. Lightly dust the dampened windscreen with a small amount of cerium oxide powder.
  1. Using a lint-free cloth, rub the cerium oxide powder across the screen so that it forms a paste on contact with the wet windscreen. Starting from the top-left portion of the windscreen, the powder should be rubbed vigorously across the surface of the windscreen using a circular motion. Work the powder/paste from left to right at the top of the windscreen and then continue working downwards until the entire surface of the windscreen has been evenly covered in the cerium oxide paste.
  1. Most of the cerium oxide dust/paste will be removed by this action, however, any remaining dust/paste residue should be removed from the windscreen surface by rinsing thoroughly with clean water.
  1. To confirm the windscreen has been thoroughly and effectively cleaned, apply water to the surface of the windscreen and note how the water behaves in contact with the glass surface. If the windscreen has been effectively cleaned, the water should coat the surface of the windscreen glass in an even and continuous film (as shown on the left-side of the windscreen pictured below). If the water forms into individual droplets (beads) on the surface of the glass (as shown on the right-side of the windscreen pictured below), this indicates that the cleaning process in incomplete and that waxy or silicone residues may still be covering the surface of the windscreen glass. In this case, repeat steps 2-5 above.
  1. Once the windscreen has been cleaned appropriately, the protective paper roll should be removed from the wiper blades.
  1. Return the vehicle to the customer and advise the customer as to best practice for ensuring a clean windscreen, i.e.: to avoid using vehicle cleaning products which contain a wax treatment on the windscreen and other windows.
