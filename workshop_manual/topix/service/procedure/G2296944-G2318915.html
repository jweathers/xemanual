Adhesives, Fixings and Consumables

Through extensive research, Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) has identified the most appropriate method to rectify aftermarket damage. It is key to safety and vehicle integrity that only the approved methods, materials and equipment are used. To successfully complete structural repairs to JLR products, it is necessary for the repairs to follow the correct aftermarket process for adhesive (bonding), riveting and welding to ensure vehicle integrity is maintained after a collision.

Bonding

This section provides information on the bonded joints used in repair. The following topics are covered:

  • Equipment.
  • Surface preparation.
  • Bonding Application.

It is a pre-requisite that any person undertaking any repairs, which involve panel bonding, has attended Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) Approved Training and has achieved the correct skill level to undertake these processes. Bonding is classed as a Category 'A' repair.

3M 08115

Equipment

The approved bonding equipment consists of:

  • Approved two-pack adhesive applicator.

Surface Preparation

Pre-treating the panels to be bonded, as described in this section, promotes the improved adhesion of the bonding adhesive.

CAUTION:

Bonding without pre-treatment will reduce the performance of the joint.

  • Remove any original adhesive, E-Coat primer (new panels) and paint from the areas to be bonded or welded using a fine bristle disc.
  • Wash the surface with soap and water to remove water-soluble contaminants. Follow the soap and water wash with an appropriate VOC compliant product for removal of surface contaminants.
  • Straighten all metal, and “dry-fit” the parts.
  • Clamp the part in place and check for fit and alignment.
  • Remove the panel from the vehicle. All areas to be welded should be coated with Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) approved 'weld-thru' coating. Adhesive should not be applied to these areas.

Bonding Application

The approved bonding adhesive is supplied in a two-pack cartridge.

A two-pack cartridge gun with a disposable mixing nozzle is used for the application of the adhesive.

  • Apply an adhesive bead to the prepared surfaces of both pieces to be bonded.
  • Using a plastic spreader or acid brush, tool out the adhesive to cover all bare surfaces.
  • Apply a 5 mm "zig zag" bead directly to either the replacement panel or vehicle structure.
  • Make sure a continuous bead of adhesive surrounds fixing holes.
NOTES:

  • On completion, the mixing nozzle must be discarded. Make sure an air tight seal is provided for the cartridges.

  • The following technical information and data should be considered representative or typical only.

The following times have been determined with ambient air temperature and substrate temperature @ 70°F (21°C) and are considered typical values.

  • Work Time-90 Minutes
  • Clamp Time-4 Hours
  • Cure Time-24 Hours
NOTE:

Panel Bonding Adhesive is a two-part epoxy adhesive which provides extended work-time but can be rapidly cured with heat.

3M Impact Resistant Structural Adhesive - 07333

Surface Preparation

  • Remove any original adhesive, or E-Coat from new panels.
  • Clean the surface to be treated using approved pre-cleaner/degreaser.
NOTE:

No pyrosil required with this adhesive.

Bonding application

  • Apply an adhesive bead to the prepared surfaces of both pieces to be bonded.
  • Using an acid brush, tool out the adhesive to cover all bare surfaces.
  • Apply a 5 mm "zig zag" bead directly to either the replacement panel or vehicle structure.
  • Apply a large enough bead to allow the adhesive to fill all voids and squeeze out of the flange seam, indicating that the joint is completely sealed.
  • Make sure a continuous bead of adhesive surrounds fixing holes.
NOTE:

Set fasteners while the adhesive is still uncured, typically within 2 hours of adhesive application.

Fixings

There are four groups of fixings used, other than conventional nuts and bolts. It is important that the correct procedures are followed for the removal and replacement of these fixings and, where applicable, the correct tools are used.

  • Self-Piercing Rivet (SPR)
  • Breakstem Fastener (BSF)
  • Friction Drilling Screw (FDS)
  • Earth Stud

Self-Piercing Rivet (SPR)

Self-Piercing Rivets (SPRs) are available in various sizes dependent on the size of the stack of panels to which they are fitted. A stack refers to the combined gauge of the panels being riveted.

CAUTION:

Operating the Self Piercing Rivet (SPR) installation tool with no SPR or material in position will damage the tool Die.

Removal

Align the plunger and anvil over the fixing and depress the trigger. Depressing the trigger will allow the plunger to act on the fixing and remove it from the panel.

Where tool access is impossible, drill out the remnant using a 5.3mm Cryobit drill bit. Remove all debris.

Self-piercing removal tools can be used with an optional, larger C frame for improved access. Installation of the new rivet should be located close to the original rivet location and not in the centre to preserve land for further repairs. The same process is followed for any subsequent replacements. Where access for the tool is restricted and cannot be used, or there is insufficient space on the panel, the fixings should be replaced with Breakstem Fasteners, unless the repair method dictates otherwise.

Installation

NOTES:

  • Prior to replacing a self-piercing fixing, a test should be performed using identical panel materials. Make sure that all settings are correct and an acceptable joint has been achieved.

  • Make sure the Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) approved SPRs, die type and load are used during installation.

Load new fixing into the Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) approved tool. Align the tool over the fixing position and depress the trigger. As the trigger is depressed, the tool will clamp itself onto the work piece and install the fixing.

Breakstem Fasteners (BSF)

Breakstem Fasteners are used in repair if the joint/stack cannot be accessed from both sides or require a greater load.

  • Breakstem fasteners will cope with joint/stack sizes of 1.5 mm to 12 mm.
  • A pre-drilled 6.5 mm hole will be required before fitment.
  • To identify the correct size and part number of structural breakstem fasteners to be used in repair always refer to TOPlx.

Breakstem Fastener (BSF)

Breakstem Fasteners have the following benefits:

  • Exceptional shear and tensile strength
  • Large blind side bearing area against the rear sheet
  • Interference lock formed by a splined stem
  • Spreads the tail bearing/clamp load on the rear sheet
  • Provides strong, vibration resistant joints
  • No loose stems

When the repair procedure requires the fitment of a breakstem fastener the stack size is identified within the repair procedures (millimetres).

Removal

Breakstem Fasteners (BSFs) used in production are removed by removing the centre of the fixing using a 4 mm punch. Once the centre has been removed, drill out the remnant using a 6.5 mm Cryobit drill bit. Remove all debris.

NOTE:

Aftermarket Breakstem Fasteners (BSFs) are removed in the same way as BSFs used in production.

Installation

Make sure a Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) approved installation tool is used during the repair process. Prior to replacement, make sure the fixing hole has been drilled to 6.5mm, using a Cryobit drill bit and all debris has been removed.

Check that you have the correct size Breakstem Fastener (BSF) for replacement and insert it into the JLR approved tool. Use the weight of the tool to apply light pressure as the BSF is inserted into its hole. Depress the trigger, which will tighten the BSF in the hole, once a pre-determined pressure is reached the wasted stud will be ejected into the tool.

Friction Drilling Screws (FDS)

NOTE:

When M5 Friction Drilling Screws (FDS) are removed in service, they must be replaced with a new larger M6 FDS.

This process is also called thermal drilling, flow drilling, form drilling, or friction stir drilling.

This joining method enables high-quality assembly of thin sheet metals made of steel and aluminium. The fixing is pressed against a target material with both high rotational speed and high pressure. This process produces a high local production of heat, which softens the object. The excess material forms a sleeve, which makes it possible to create very strong threaded joint in thin material.

  • The main benefits of FDS is one-sided accessibility for application and screw removability.
  • The screw joint is able to transfer high pull-out forces and high shearing strength.

Removal

  • Screw out the M5 FDS using a battery operated tool or hand tools.
  • Clean the area and remove any debris using industrial vacuum cleaner.

Installation

  • Position the new M6 FDS in the original position.
  • Screw in the FDS until the required torque is reached.
NOTE:

Install the Friction Drilling Screw (FDS) screw using 2-4 Nm of torque until flush with the panel, then apply additional 4 Nm of torque (8-10 Nm total). Overall torque should not exceed 11 Nm.

Earth Stud

Secure and reliable earthing is imperative for trouble free function of electronic components and management systems. A solid permanent joint is needed for long term stability under load, even with high short term amperage values and corresponding heat generation.

The earth bond comprises two components:

  • A conical dowel having a male thread.
  • A cylindrical flanged bush.

A tensile force is applied to the dowel using a hand hydraulic tool with a pre-determined pulling force. As the dowel is pulled through the bush, the bush remains fixed in the structure and expands within the hole making electrical contact. The tool completes its cycle resulting in a permanent electrical connection.

Removal/Preparation

Damaged or broken studs:

  • Locate the broken or cross-threaded stud.
  • Dress the broken stud to a safe and acceptable repair standard.
  • Carry out drilling in close proximity to the broken /cross-threaded stud.

Installation

  • Measure and mark stud location.
  • Using a stepped cutter, drill a hole in the mounting plate to a predetermined diameter depending on the material and the stack thickness.
  • Screw the earth bond stud into the nose of the applicator gun. The flange of the stud should be flush with the panel surface.
  • Place the new stud into the drilled hole. Apply pressure to the trigger of the applicator gun until audible click is heard. This will indicate the applicator gun has reached the end of its cycle.

Glenair earth bonding system is the approved equipment to repair the earth studs on Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) vehicles.
