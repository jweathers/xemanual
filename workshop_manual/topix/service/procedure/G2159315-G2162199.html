Wiring Harnesses

DESCRIPTION AND OPERATION

Overview

Where damage to a vehicle or a wiring harness has occurred; it can be more cost effective to repair the wiring harness with an overlay or a lower level assembly, rather than replacing a full wiring harness. This is especially the case where pre-terminated leads or other wiring harness repair kits are not available.

Description

The overlays and lower level assemblies are available from the parts catalogue and include such items as;

  • Antenna leads and other special cables
  • Front corner repair harnesses
  • Trunk / tailgate harnesses
  • Front and rear bumper harnesses

CAUTIONS:

  • Any damaged cable / assemblies must not be repaired and must be replaced.

  • The replacement overlay must follow the original wiring harness route, to avoid the risk of introducing electrical interference issues.

NOTES:

  • The original cable / assembly must left be in the wiring harness and the overlay taped to the wiring harness along the original route.

  • Where possible, when installing a replacement cable / assembly, cut off the unused ends of the damaged cable / assembly at their exit points from the host harness and discard.

  • The cut ends must be free from sharp edges. Any strands of wire must be taped over, to prevent damaging adjacent wires or cables or any personal injury.

  • The replacement cable / assembly must be attached with coverings to replicate the existing installation of the damaged cable / assembly.

  • If the cut end of the cable / assembly is in an area of the vehicle, which is exposed to moisture, i.e. the engine bay; Cover the cut end with glue-lined heatshrink, squeezed with pliers to seal the end and prevent moisture ingress.
