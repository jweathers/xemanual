Rear Floor Panel Reinforcement Outer (G2263840)

Removal
NOTE:

In this procedure the rear floor panel reinforcement outer is installed in conjunction with:

  • Rear bumper
  • Rear armature
  • Rear bumper mounting
  • Battery
  • Luggage compartment floor trim panel
  • Luggage compartment trim panels
  • Back panel outer
  • Back panel inner
  • spare wheel well
  • Exhaust system
  • Fuel tank
  • Rear subframe.

  1. The rear floor panel reinforcement outer is serviced as indicated.
  2. Before commencing this procedure make sure that you are aware of all Health and Safety requirements.
    Refer to: Health and Safety Precautions (100-00 General Information, Description and Operation).
  3. NOTES:

    • Make sure a zinc rich primer is applied to any bare metal surfaces.

    • Any joints sealed as part of vehicle manufacture must be sealed as part of the repair.

    • To make sure that the vehicle is correctly aligned it must be placed on a Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) approved alignment jig.

    When installing the rear floor panel reinforcement outer make sure that you are aware of the methods, tools and fixings used in this procedure.
    Refer to: Body Repairs (501-25A Body Repairs - General Information, Description and Operation).

  16. Release and position the wiring harness to one side.
  17. Remove the spot welds as indicated.
  18. Carefully separate the joints and remove the rear floor panel reinforcement outer.
Installation
  1. Dress the flanges where necessary.
  2. Offer up the new rear floor panel reinforcement outer and clamp into position. Check alignment, if correct proceed to next step, if not rectify and check the alignment again before proceeding.
  3. Drill holes where the Metal Active Gas (MAG) plug welds are to be installed as indicated.
  4. Remove the new rear floor panel reinforcement outer.
  5. Deburr the drilled holes.
  6. Clean and prepare the panel surfaces.
  7. Offer up the new rear floor panel reinforcement outer and clamp into position. Check alignment, if correct proceed to next step, if not rectify and check the alignment again before proceeding.
  8. Install the MAG plug welds as indicated.
  9. Dress all welds.
  10. Make sure that any open or exposed panel joints are correctly sealed.
  11. Make sure corrosion protection is applied to all areas affected by the repair.
  12. The installation of associated panels and components is the reversal of the removal procedure.
